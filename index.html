Tax Filing

ITR Filing Guide

Step-by-step guide to file your Income Tax Return online.

1

Gather Documents

Collect Form 16 (Part A & B) from your employer, bank interest certificates, investment proofs (80C/80D), Form 26AS, and AIS from the Income Tax Portal.

Download Form 26AS ↗
2

Register / Login to e-Filing Portal

Visit the official Income Tax e-Filing portal. Register with your PAN if you're a first-time filer.

Go to e-Filing Portal ↗
3

Choose ITR Form

For salaried individuals with one employer and no capital gains: use ITR-1 (Sahaj). With multiple employers or capital gains use ITR-2.

4

Pre-fill & Verify Details

The portal pre-fills salary, TDS and investments from Form 16 and AIS. Verify all figures carefully against your documents.

5

Compute Tax & Choose Regime

Use our Old vs New Tax Regime Calculator to choose the most beneficial regime before filing.

6

e-Verify & Submit

Submit the form and e-verify using Aadhaar OTP, net banking, or DSC. ITR is not complete until e-verified within 30 days.

e-Verify Return ↗
1

Applicable Forms

Business/self-employed individuals with income from business or profession use ITR-3 (with books) or ITR-4 (Sugam) for presumptive taxation under 44AD/44ADA/44AE.

2

Collect Financial Statements

Prepare P&L, balance sheet, GST returns (GSTR-3B/1), TDS certificates (26AS), and advance tax payment challans.

3

Tax Audit (if applicable)

If turnover exceeds ₹1 crore (business) or ₹50 lakh (profession), a tax audit under Sec 44AB is mandatory. Upload audit report before filing.

4

Filing Deadline

For non-audit cases: July 31. For tax audit cases: October 31. Late filing attracts penalty u/s 234F up to ₹5,000.

5

Submit & e-Verify

Login to the e-filing portal, choose the correct ITR form, enter income details, compute tax, pay any dues, submit and e-verify.

e-Filing Portal ↗
1

Identify Capital Gains

Capital gains arise from sale of equity shares, mutual funds, property, bonds etc. Use our Capital Gains Calculator.

2

STCG vs LTCG

Equity funds/shares held <12 months: STCG @ 15%. Held >12 months: LTCG @ 10% above ₹1 lakh. Debt funds: taxed as income (post April 2023 amendment).

3

Indexation (for property/debt)

For LTCG on real estate and pre-2023 debt funds, apply CII (Cost Inflation Index) to reduce indexed cost. Find CII on the IT portal.

4

Use ITR-2

Salaried with capital gains must use ITR-2. Enter each transaction in Schedule CG. P&L statement from broker / Zerodha tax report simplifies this.

5

Set-off & Carry Forward

STCL can offset STCG/LTCG. LTCL can only offset LTCG. Unabsorbed losses can be carried forward for 8 years.
